Glossary

All the lexicon of feminism.

A

Amazons
Legendary women's people from Greek mythology. Fighting as equals with their male counterparts.
Abortion
Premature interruption of pregnancy, it is authorized in France since the Veil law enacted on January 17, 1975.
Agression sexuelle
Atteinte sexuelle commise avec violence, surpise, contrainte ou menace.

B

Bas-bleuisme
Pejorative terms for women writers of the 19th century.

C

Catcall
Unsolicited questioning in the public space of a female person.
Cisgenre
A person whose gender identity corresponds to the sex they were born with.
Collage
Action of sticking posters on the public highway to spread a message. A method widely used by feminists.

D

Desir
Conscious and self-induced tendency to pleasure.
Declaration of Women's Rights
To be in a position to impose one's will on others.

E

Emancipation
Action of obtaining freedom by freeing oneself from someone's authority.
Excision
Ritual removal of the clitoris (clitoridectomy) or foreskin (circumcision).
Equality
Absence of discrimination between individuals.

F

Feminisme
Activist movements fighting for equal rights between men and women.
Feminicide
Murder of a woman, a girl because of her sex. (In France 152 were killed by the blows of their companions or ex-companions during the year 2019).
Feminist wave
Current periodization of the history of feminism characterized by specific demands and objectives. There are three of them, and more and more a fourth is becoming popular.

G

Girl Power
Anglicism used in reference to an attitude of independence, confidence and empowerment of women.

H

Harassment
Repeated violence against a person.
Hypersexualization
ocietal trend giving more and more place to sexuality. Ex: the sexualization of teenagers (often women) for the promotion of a product.

I

Inequalities
When a person or group engages in practices, holds resources, or has access to goods and services that others cannot.

K

Kyriarchie
Social structure based on domination, oppression and submission.

L

La Malédiction d'être fille
Book realizing the report of the violence made to the woman in the world. Written by Dominique Sigaud in 2019.

M

Machismo
Ideology advocating the superiority of men.
Mansplaining
Explanation by a man, to a woman, in a way that is considered condescending.
Matriarchy
Societal regime where women have more decision-making power than men.
Misogynist
Contempt (usually male) for women.
Mixed
A group of individuals from various groups. (Gender, sexual orientation, ethnicity).
Menstrual cycle
Period of preparation of the female body for fertilization The cycle starts on the first day of menstruation, and lasts between 3 and 5 days.

O

Opression
To overwhelm a person with authority.

P

Parity
Not distinguishing between individuals
Patriarchate
Societal regime characterized by the domination of the male sex over women.
Prostitution
The act of exchanging one's body for sex in exchange for money.
Phallocratie
Domination of men over women (symbolism of the phallus).
Post Feminisme
Feminist movements coming after the traditional feminism of the 1970s.

Q

Queers
Person whose sexual orientation or identity does not correspond to the dominant models. (Male, Female, Straight sexual, Homo sexual) ex: Non binary.

R

Rape culture
Sociological concept within a society, minimizing, normalizing or even encouraging rape
Rape
Act by which a person forces another to have sexual relations with him, by violence, constraint or surprise

S

Safe-space
Anglicism for an environment in which a person from a minority background can be confident that they will not be discriminated against.
Sexe
Biological conformation that distinguishes the man from the woman.
Sexocide
Murder of a person because of their sex.
Sexism
Attitude of discrimination based on gender.
Stereotype
Ready-made opinion reducing the particularities.
